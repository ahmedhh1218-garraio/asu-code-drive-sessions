◆ The big idea

No hand-coded rules — it learns by watching you drive

You drive the car a few laps from your phone; it records what it senses and what you do, then a tiny neural network learns to copy you. Three stages:

1 · Demonstrate

You drive. Sensors + your actions are recorded together.

➜

2 · Train

A laptop turns the log into a tiny .tflite brain.

➜

3 · Self-drive

The Pi runs the model in real time and steers itself.

◆ How we'll work together

This is run like a real engineering team

Not a lecture course — every session follows the same loop.

STEP 01 · at home

I give you keywords

At the end of each session you leave with a short list of keywords to research.

STEP 02 · at home

You search

You look them up — datasheets, docs, articles — and build your own understanding.

STEP 03 · at home

You prepare

You put together what you found and get ready to explain it to the team.

STEP 04 · in the room

You present

You present; we discuss; I add the industry context — then we build it on the hardware.

🏭 From the field

This mirrors how embedded teams actually operate

In industry nobody hands you a finished answer. You get a problem and some keywords, you research the datasheet and the kernel docs, you propose an approach, your peers challenge it in review, and only then do you touch the hardware. That's the habit that makes you employable.
